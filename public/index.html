Module 1: Medicare Basics

Learning Objectives:

  • Define Medicare Parts A, B, C, and D
  • Understand eligibility and enrollment periods
  • Master key Medicare terminology

Reading Material

Key Concepts

  • Part A: Hospital insurance (inpatient)
  • Part B: Medical insurance (doctor visits, outpatient)
  • Part C: Medicare Advantage (private alternative)
  • Part D: Prescription drug coverage

In-Class Activity

Discuss with a partner: What are the main differences between Parts A and B?

Homework

Read Chapter 2 of the textbook and prepare a 2-minute summary.

Facilitator Note: Allocate 15 minutes for this module. Use a poll in Zoom to check comprehension: "Which part covers hospital stays?" Expected answer: Part A.

Module 3: Compliance & Marketing

Learning Objectives:

  • Know CMS regulations for Medicare marketing
  • Identify compliant and non-compliant messaging
  • Understand documentation and disclosure requirements

Do's and Don'ts

‚úÖ DO:

  • Provide accurate information about plan costs and benefits
  • Disclose enrollment restrictions (if any)
  • Maintain records of all marketing materials
  • Get written consent before using comparative advertising

‚ùå DON'T:

  • Make misleading claims about plan benefits
  • Pressure seniors into enrollment
  • Omit enrollment period restrictions
  • Use unapproved marketing materials

Case Study

"Our Medicare Advantage plan covers everything ‚Äî no copays, no deductibles, unlimited doctor visits!"

üö® Problem: Overly broad claims without disclaimers. Would violate CMS rules.

‚úÖ Correction: "Our Medicare Advantage plan includes preventive care with no copay, and has an annual out-of-pocket maximum."

Role-Play Activity

Setup: Breakout rooms (2‚Äì3 per group). Rotate roles: salesperson, prospect, observer.

Scenario: Prospect is skeptical about switching from Original Medicare. They're concerned about provider choice.

Time: 5 minutes per role, then debrief (2 minutes).

Facilitator Note: Provide objection cards in the chat:
  • "I like my doctor. Can I see them with this plan?"
  • "Is this going to cost me more?"
  • "I'm worried about losing coverage."

Facilitator Guide

Tips for running engaging live sessions via Zoom or Microsoft Teams.

Pre-Session Setup

  • Upload course materials to your learning management system (LMS) or shared drive.
  • Test Zoom/Teams breakout rooms (for role-play activities).
  • Prepare a slide deck (optional; use this website as the primary resource).
  • Notify participants of the Zoom/Teams link and login credentials.

Session Structure (per 90-min module)

  • 10 min: Welcome & check-in. Recap previous module.
  • 25 min: Teach core content. Use polls frequently.
  • 30 min: Interactive activity (breakout rooms, case study, role-play).
  • 15 min: Debrief. Share key takeaways.
  • 10 min: Assign homework & preview next module.

Engagement & Gamification

  • Points: Award 10 points for each quiz question answered correctly.
  • Leaderboard: Share leaderboard at the end of each session to motivate competition.
  • Badges: (Future) Award digital badges for completing modules or achieving milestones.

Zoom/Teams Tips

  • Polls: Use built-in polls for quick comprehension checks.
  • Chat: Encourage Q&A in the chat. Assign a co-facilitator to monitor.
  • Breakout Rooms: For role-play, create rooms of 2‚Äì3. Set a timer (use the facilitator timer below).
  • Recording: Record sessions (with participant consent) and share afterward.
  • Attendee List: Use it to track participation and award bonus points.

Handling Common Scenarios

  • Low engagement: Use more polls and breakout rooms. Call on individuals by name.
  • Technical issues: Have a co-facilitator monitor the chat. Offer phone call-in option.
  • Advanced learners: Assign them as "breakout room observers" or peer coaches.
  • Struggling learners: Offer 1-on-1 office hours after the session.
